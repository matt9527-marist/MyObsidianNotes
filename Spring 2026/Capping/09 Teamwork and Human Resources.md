## Teaming 
• Projects would be so much simpler if I worked by myself!  
• As a project manager, you own the project but you might  
not directly have control over the resources.  
• How do you influence your team to focus on the project?  
• You might think teaming will be easier in the professional  
world; I thought that too; now there are many priorities –  
family issues, sickness, special projects, normal  
assignments, personality conflicts, availability of  
specialized staff, etc.  

**Project Manager's Responsibilities**
• Determine what resources are needed  
• Negotiate with resource or functional managers for optimal  
available resources  
• Confirm availability of resources assigned  
• Create a project team directory  
• Create project job descriptions for team members and other  
stakeholders  
• Assign roles and responsibilities  
• Determine training needs of personnel  
• Human resource plan – how teams will be involved  
• Team member performance reports  
• Letters of commendation to team members and their bosses  
• Create a recognition and reward system

**Planning Human Resources**
• Environmental factors  
	– What organizations are involved in the project?  
	– Are there hidden agendas?  
	– Is there anyone who does not want the project?  
• Would you want a prison in your neighborhood?  
• Types of teams  
	– Dedicated – team members work full time and exclusively on the project  
	– Part-time (Matrix) – team members spend a portion of their time on the  
	project and other time on something else  
	– Partnership – several organizations work together  
		• Advantage cost savings; disadvantage one organization could back out  
	– Virtual – creates schedule, language, and/or cultural challenges  
	Team and Communication

**Building and Leading a Project Team**
• Select the right team members  
	– Identify the skills needed for the project  
	– Validate required skills and available resources  
	– Develop skills where needed  
• Organize the team  
	– Identity all sub project managers  
	– Prepare & organize team charter  
	– Establish delegation levels  
• Ensure open communication  
	– Define a communication plan  
• Inspire team members to do their best  
• Incorporate new team members as they join the project during its life cycle  
• Consider the aspects of the multiple cultures involved  
	– Social customs  
	– Time zone differences  
	– English as a second language  
	– Cultural differences
