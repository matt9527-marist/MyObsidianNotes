**Procurement**
• Project Management Plan  
	– Helps us understand where procurement is needed (i.e. do we have the plumbing skills or do we need to hire  
a plumber). Also known as Make or Buy decision  
• Requirements documentation  
	– Describes the scope of work we are procuring  
• Activity resource requirements  
	– What skills do we need to procure  
• Enterprise environmental factors  
	– Culture and existing systems – would the seller (we hire) fit well with the organization  
• Organizational process assets  
	– Templates and standards that the organization has already in place for procurement  
• Risk register  
	– What are the risks associated with the parts we are procuring; does the procurement mitigate or create new risks  
• Stakeholder register  
	– Who are we talking to and their roles; you would not want to negotiate a contract with a non-decision maker  
• Any procurements already in place on the project  
	– How many interfaces are already in pace  
• The project schedule  
	– Provides schedule constraints, required completion dates, and time estimates  
• Initial cost estimates for the work to be procured  
	– What do we think it will cost us to procure – this gives us an idea when sellers come in with their prices

• Plan Purchases and Acquisitions
	– Criteria for outside procurement
	– Make-or-buy decisions
	– Statement of Work for contract
• Plan Contracting
	– Invitation for Bids (IFBs), Request for Quotes (RFQs), Request for
Proposals (RFPs), Tender Notices, etc.
	– Evaluation criteria
• Request Supplier (Seller) Responses
	– Advertising, Bidder Conferences
• Select Suppliers
	– Price
	– Single/multiple source
	– Added value
	– Contract T’s & C’s (Terms and Conditions)
• Will guide contractor’s management processes
• Administer Contracts
	– T’s & C’s
	– Change Requests

**Contracts**
• Five Elements of a Contract  
	– Offer  
	– Acceptance  
	– Consideration (Exchange)  
	– Capacity  
	– Legality  
• Three Phases:  
	– Pre-acquisition  
	– Acquisition  
	– Post Acquisition  
• Types of Contract  
– Fixed Price  
– Cost-Reimbursable (Cost Plus)  
– Time and Material (T&M)  
• Governed by the Uniform Commercial Code (UCC)
